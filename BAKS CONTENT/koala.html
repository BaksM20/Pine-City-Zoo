KOALA

The koala is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia. Koalas are known for their fluffy ears, spoon-shaped noses, and love of eucalyptus leaves.

They spend most of their time sleeping in trees and are mostly nocturnal.

Fun fact: Koalas have fingerprints that are almost indistinguishable from humans! They can sleep up to 18-20 hours a day to conserve energy.

Conservation note: Koalas face threats from habitat destruction, disease, and climate change. Conservation efforts focus on preserving eucalyptus forests and supporting koala health.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

The koalas are located in the eucalyptus grove section of the zoo.
