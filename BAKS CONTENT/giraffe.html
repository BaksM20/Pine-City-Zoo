GIRAFFE

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, famous for its long neck and legs. Giraffes are native to Africa and are known for their gentle nature and unique appearance.

Giraffes feed mainly on leaves from tall trees, especially acacias, and can spot predators from far away due to their height. Their long tongues and prehensile lips help them grasp leaves and twigs.

Each giraffe has a unique pattern of spots, much like a human fingerprint. They live in loose herds and are generally peaceful animals.
