WARTHOG

The warthog is a wild member of the pig family found in grassland, savanna, and woodland in sub-Saharan Africa. Warthogs are known for their distinctive tusks and facial warts.

They are tough, adaptable animals that can survive in harsh environments and are often seen kneeling while grazing.

Fun fact: Warthogs use their tusks and snouts to dig for food and create burrows. They often back into their burrows to face outward, ready to defend against predators!

Conservation note: Warthogs are not currently endangered, but they rely on healthy grasslands and water sources. Protecting their habitats helps ensure their survival.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

The warthogs are located near the open plains area of the zoo.
